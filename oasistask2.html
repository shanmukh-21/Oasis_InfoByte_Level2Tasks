Vikram Ambalal Sarabhai

12th Aug 1919- 30th Dec 1971

Biography:
Vikram Ambalal Sarabhai(12th August 1919 - 30th December 1971) was an Indian Physicist and astronomer who initiated space research and helped develop nuclear power in India. He was honoured with Padma Bhushan in 1966 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1972. He is internationally regarded as the "Father of Indian Space Program."

Distinguished Positions:
  • President of the Physics Section, Indian Science Congress(1962)
  • President of the General Conference of the I.E.I.A Vienna(1970)
  • Chairman of the Atomic Energy Commission of India(1966-1971)
Legacy:
  • The Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre, which is the Indian SPace Research Organization's lead facility for launch vechile development located in Thiruvananthapuram, is named in his memory
  • The lander on India's moon mission Chandrayaan-2 which was to land near the south pole of the moon on 20th September 2019 was named as Vikram in his honour.
